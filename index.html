Malaysia's Population Portrait in 2024





By clicking on a state in the map above, the population breakdown of males and females is displayed in the donut chart.

Interestingly, in almost every state, the males population is larger than the females population.

Additionally, by clicking on the slice of donut chart, it will filter the gender population by each age group in bar chart on the right.

The bar chart shows the population distribution across different age groups for each state. In most states, the age groups of 20-24, 25-29, and 30-34 have larger populations compared to other age groups, reflecting a strong presence of working-age individuals. Suprisingly, Terengganu stands out with the 0-4 age group having the largest population, suggesting a younger demographic and possibly higher birth rates.

Remarkably, as the age increases, the female population tends to surpass the male population, indicating a shift in demographic patterns in older age groups.


Shifting Borders: Nationality Trends in Immigration (2013-2023)

The chart on the left compares the population rankings of the top 10 countries of origin for residents from overseas in Malaysia, based on the 2023 census.

By comparing with previous census results, it is evident that residents from Singapore and India have consistently ranked first and second, respectively, ahead of other countries.

In 2023, the residents from Thailand come out on top among overseas residents in Malaysia, boasting a population of 1,551 thousand, surpassing that of China, which has around 1,441 thousand residents.

Notably, South Korea experienced the fastest rise in rankings, jumping three places since the last census interval in 2018. Meanwhile, Vietnam has also seen steady growth, climbing from tenth to eighth place in recent years.



Life and Loss: Insights into Population Dynamics



The chart above illustrates the trends in natural population increase over time. In 2021, there was a significant drop in the natural increase, primarily attributed to a surge in deaths, which reached 224,569. This drop is particularly concerning as it highlights the ongoing impact of health crises on population dynamics.

To identify the cause of this decline, we turn to the exploded bar chart on the left, which reveals that COVID-19 infections were the leading contributor, resulting in 31,063 deaths, accounting for nearly 19.8% of the total deaths.

Next, we examine which areas faced the most severe COVID-19 infections in 2021. The chart below indicates that regions with higher population density experienced greater total deaths. This correlation suggests that densely populated areas, such as Labuan and Kuala Lumpur, were more susceptible to widespread outbreaks due to the close proximity of residents.
